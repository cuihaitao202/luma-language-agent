Most adults do not need another language course. They need a patient teacher inside the life they already have. Luma turns the next real moment in your day into a lesson your brain can use.

I choose one of eight languages, tell Luma what I need language for, and choose a three-minute daily rhythm. There is no placement test. Luma adapts from what I understand and say.

My meeting starts soon, so Luma prepares a coffee-shop moment. First, I listen for meaning instead of translating every word. This develops the ability adults actually need under pressure: recognizing the speaker's intention.

Then I say what I would genuinely order. Luma is designed to make speaking feel safe from the first minute. If microphone access is not available, the entire experience remains testable with the typing path.

GPT-5.6 Terra receives the transcript through a secure server-side Responses API call. It recognizes that I communicated successfully, then chooses only one high-value refinement. Luma never turns a useful attempt into a wall of red corrections.

The expression is now connected to an intention, a situation, and successful use. That is a stronger memory unit than an isolated vocabulary word.

Tomorrow, “Could I get” returns inside a meeting as a request for clarification. The change of context is the point: usable transfer, not short-term memorization.

I built Luma with Codex during OpenAI Build Week. Codex helped turn the adult-learning problem into this product mechanism, implemented the responsive experience and secure Cloudflare worker, integrated GPT-5.6 structured outputs, and ran the build, deployment, and end-to-end validation loop.

Luma makes the learner's life the curriculum: hear it, say it, refine one thing, and meet it again before it disappears.
